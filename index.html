What is Raw Honey?

Our Himalayan Raw Honey: Health Bene-fits, Uses & Floral Varieties Himalayan raw honey is a pure, unprocessed natural sweetener harvested from beehives in the pristine Himalayan region. Unlike processed honey, it is not heated or ultra-filtered, allowing it to retain natural enzymes, pollen, antioxidants, and essential nutrients. Thanks to the diverse Himalayan flora and high-altitude environment, Himalayan raw honey offers superior quality, taste, and health benefits. Health Benefits of Himalayan Raw Honey 1. Rich in Natural Nutrients Himalayan raw honey contains natural sugars (fructose and glucose), amino acids, vitamins (B-complex, C, and E), minerals, and enzymes such as catalase and superoxide dismutase. These nutrients help boost energy, immunity, and overall health. 2. High Antioxidant Properties Loaded with flavonoids and phenolic compounds, Himalayan raw honey helps fight free radicals, reduce oxidative stress, and support healthy ageing. Darker varieties often provide stronger antioxidant activity. 3. Natural Antibacterial & Antimicrobial Himalayan raw honey has proven antibacterial and antimicrobial properties due to its acidic pH and hy-drogen peroxide content. It helps fight infections, supports wound healing, and strengthens the immune system. 4. Supports Digestion & Gut Health Regular consumption of Himalayan raw honey helps soothe the digestive tract, promotes gut health, re-duces acidity, and improves nutrient absorption. It is commonly consumed with warm water or ginger for better digestion. 5. Supports Heart, Brain & Skin Health The flavonoids in Himalayan raw honey support cardiovascular health, improve memory and cognitive function, moisturise skin, and strengthen hair naturally. Different Floral Varieties of Himalayan Raw Honey The health benefits and flavour of Himalayan raw honey depend on the floral source from which bees collect nectar: Himalayan Multiflora Honey – Sourced from diverse wildflowers; ideal for daily immunity and overall wellness. Litchi Blossom Honey – Light, aromatic honey that supports digestion and boosts energy. Mustard Flower Honey – Strong flavour; traditionally used for respiratory health and joint support. Wild Forest Honey – Dark, mineral-rich honey known for high antioxidant and antimicrobial properties. Acacia Honey – Mild and light; suitable for diabetics in moderation due to its low glycaemic index. Rhododendron Honey – Rare Himalayan variety valued for its soothing and anti-inflammatory effects. Other Honey types may vary from flower to flower where beehive are placed to produce honey. Some other popular varieties includes, Ajwain honey, Jamun Honey, Anar Honey, Tulsi Honey, Babul Honey, eu-calyptus Honey and many more. Raw Honey vs Processed Honey

Unlike processed honey, raw honey retains its natural enzymes, vitamins, minerals, and pollen. Processed honey is often heated and filtered, which can remove many of these beneficial compounds.

Uses of Raw Honey

  • Sweetener for tea and coffee
  • Ingredient in cooking and baking
  • Natural skincare and face masks
  • Home remedies for coughs and colds

Storage Tips

Store raw honey in a cool, dry place at room temperature. Crystallization is natural and does not mean the honey has gone bad.
